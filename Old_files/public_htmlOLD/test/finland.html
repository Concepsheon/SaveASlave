Finland

The Republic of Finland is a country located in northeastern Europe. The capital of Finland is Helsinki.  Finland has a per capita GDP of $35,900.  Over 25% of employed people in Finland work in public services, with other major occupations including commerce, industry, and finance, insurance, and business services. 

Finland has a population of 5.3 million people, with an estimated 670-740 people forced into modern slavery.  Most people trafficked in Finland are trapped in forced labor situations, in many cases in construction, restaurants, agriculture, and as domestic servants.  Other people are exploited sexually.  Finland is a transit and destination country for human trafficking; most, although not all, people trapped in slavery in Finland are from other countries.

Finland is classified as a Tier 1 country by the Trafficking in Persons Report, 2013.  This means that the Finnish government meets the minimum standards set by the Trafficking Victims Protection Act.  Finland has also implemented a new trafficking statute in 2011, allowing for increased prosecution in human trafficking offenses.

Finland provides assistance to people in human trafficking situations through the Joutseno Reception Centre.
